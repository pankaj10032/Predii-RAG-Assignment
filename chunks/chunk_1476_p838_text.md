Normal Operation The braking energy provided to the trailer is varied with a Pulse Width Modulated (PWM) signal that switches between 0 volts and battery voltage, the higher the duty cycle the more braking power available. With the vehicle stationary and the manual slider switch fully to the left with a gain of 10, there should be more than 10-12 volts supplied to pin 3 of the 7-pin trailer tow connector. The Trailer Brake Control (TBC) module also receives information from the brake pressure switch (also called the speed control deactivation switch) and the stoplamp switch from the PCM over the High Speed Controller Area Network (HS-CAN).
